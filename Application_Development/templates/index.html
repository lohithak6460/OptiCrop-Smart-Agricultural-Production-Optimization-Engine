Project Scope & Social-Business Impact

The Business Problem

Traditional farming relies heavily on generalized or legacy fertilization practices. This lack of precision leads to two major problems: soil degradation from over-fertilization, and reduced crop yield from under-fertilization. OptiCrop addresses this by pairing machine learning classification algorithms with a chemical deficiency analysis engine.

Key Business Requirements

  • Accuracy: Real-time crop recommendations with >95% confidence based on soil composition.
  • Optimization Advisor: Mathematical offsets providing custom guidelines to adjust soil parameters for target crops.
  • Clean REST Services: Ability to expose APIs for integration with smart IoT devices.

Social & Business Impact

By optimizing fertilizer consumption, OptiCrop reduces agricultural expenses by up to 25% while conserving groundwater resources from nitrate leaching. Accurate matching prevents crop failure under shifting regional climate profiles, securing food chains and stabilizing local rural economies.

Literature Survey Overview

Recent studies in precision agriculture show that ensemble models (like Random Forests) outperform standard linear classifiers by capturing complex non-linear interactions between soil properties (NPK, pH) and dynamic weather inputs (rainfall, humidity). OptiCrop utilizes a Random Forest framework reaching an evaluation accuracy of 99.55%.
